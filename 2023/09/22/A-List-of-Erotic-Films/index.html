A List of Erotic Films

Posted on 2023-09-22 Edited on 2023-09-25 In Movies Word count in article: 743 Reading time ≈ 3 mins.

I will introduce some erotic films I’ve watched, so please ensure you are old enough. I will only list films that have left a significant impression on me or that I believe are noteworthy. This list will be periodically updated, and you can revisit it at your convenience. Let’s commence.

Firstly, I would like to mention that in China, erotic films are referred to as Qing-se Pian, while pornographic films are denoted as Se-Qing Pian. As you may observe, the distinguishing factor lies in the arrangement of these words. Qing means love, whereas Se means lust. In China. There exists an ancient adage: Qing-se Pian emphasizes Qing, while Se-qing Pian emphasizes Se. Therefore, when viewing these films, one should pay particular attention to the emotional journey of the characters rather than the physical actions.

The Lover

The film is an adaptation of the 1984 novel The Lover, originally published by Les Éditions de Minuit. I encountered this novel during my high school years, following a recommendation from another author I admire, Wang Xiaobo. His portrayal of the novel was remarkably elegant and succinct. Wang firmly believed that a truly exceptional novel could be recited like poetry, and he believed that The Lover impeccably met this criterion. Consequently, prior to viewing the film, I was already intimately familiar with the entire plot. It’s regrettable that the director chose not to capture the opening chapters of the book I think.

Within the film, the roles of the male and female protagonists are portrayed by Jane March and Tony Leung, respectively. Leung assumes the character of a wealthy Chinese entrepreneur, while March embodies a financially disadvantaged French schoolgirl, both residing in Vietnam. Leung’s character becomes infatuated with the girl, who, if memory serves me correctly, is significantly younger than him. The film narrates a story encompassing the man, the girl, and their respective families, which, to be frank, it’s a little boring.

Anyway, the movie is rated R and so it’s worth watching still.

Lust, Caution

The film was directed by Oscar winner Ang Lee, a renowned figure in the Western world acclaimed for his exceptional works, notably Life of Pi and Brokeback Mountain. Lust, Caution was released in 2007, played by Tony Leung and Wei Tang.

The movie tells a story that a bunch of students tryed to assassinate a traitor called Mr. Yi. Tang plays a young and beautiful schoolgirl Wang Jiazhi who disguised as a rich lady helped by her classmates and seduced Mr. Yi to find a chance to kill him. And obviously, they are too young and unskilled to achieve the goal. After serveral years, Wang was commisioned to relaunch the plan by an underground orgnisation. With the implementation of the plan, Wang fell in love with Mr. Yi although she was raped by him for servral times. And that’s my introduction.

This movie is controversial in China mainland for many people thinking it was promoted by its sexual plot and it has been famous for this actually.

Deep End

I can’t recall how I found the film that day and frankly I hadn’t heard the actors and director. So maybe I watched it just because it’s an erotic film and I found out that the movie is much better than I expected. By the way, the heroine was one of Paul McCartney’s girlfriends.

The story is quite simple so I won’t retell it which there’s really not much to say. It is worth mentioning that Mike (the male lead) ate 7 or 9 hot dogs in a scene which made me hungry and wanted one.

After all, it’s a really simple and younthful film and they both died in the end. :)

The Handmaiden

It is indeed surprising to find The Handmaiden in IMDb’s top 250 list. I didn’t expect the film is that popular.

This film is a blend of lesbian romance and suspense, and what truly captivates is its narrative structure. The director divides the entire story into three parts, offering a comprehensive view from each of the three protagonists’ perspectives. Only by piecing together all three views can one fully grasp the whole story.

And as an erotic film, The Handmaid is a masterpiece. Moreover, I really wonder whether the books in the film actually exist or the director just made them up.
